What is BMI?

BMI is a measure of body fat based on your height and weight. It provides a rough idea of whether you're underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese, and it's used as a screening tool to identify possible weight problems in adults.

How to Calculate BMI

BMI is calculated by dividing your weight in kilograms by the square of your height in meters.
What Your BMI Means:
Underweight: BMI less than 18.5
Normal weight: BMI between 18.5 and 24.9
Overweight: BMI between 25 and 29.9
Obese: BMI 30 or higher

WHY TRACK YOUR BMI

Tracking your BMI can help you monitor your weight and assess your risk for weight-related health problems. It's a simple way to gauge whether you're in a healthy weight range.
